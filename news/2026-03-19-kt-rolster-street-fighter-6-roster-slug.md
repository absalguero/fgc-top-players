**kt Rolster** has entered the **Street Fighter 6** competitive arena on Thursday, confirming the signing of South Korean players DAKCORGI and JP KING.

**Key Takeaways:**
* **kt Rolster brings major organizational backing from the broader esports world into the FGC.**
* **DAKCORGI joins the team fresh off an impressive Capcom Cup 12 run with C. Viper.**
* **The organization is rebranding its social channels to encompass a wider esports focus.**

---

## The Legacy of kt Rolster

Many fighting game fans might be unfamiliar with the sheer scale of the kt Rolster brand. The organization is a titan in the South Korean esports landscape. Backed by the telecommunications giant KT Corporation, the team has historically dominated in games like StarCraft and League of Legends. 

Their League of Legends roster recently made a deep run to the Grand Finals of the 2025 World Championship. Expanding into the fighting game community marks a massive shift in their competitive strategy. This move brings significant resources and a massive built-in fanbase to the competitive Street Fighter ecosystem.

---

## Building a Competitive Roster

The signing of Yeon "DAKCORGI" Je-gil provides the new division with immediate veteran credibility. DAKCORGI recently competed in Capcom Cup 12 on the global stage. He delivered an incredibly impressive showing using C. Viper. His performance demonstrated an ability to compete at the highest possible level and navigate challenging tournament brackets. 

Kim "JP KING" Sung-jun joins him on the roster to complete the newly formed duo. The two players will represent the brand in upcoming major tournaments while actively engaging with fans through dedicated streaming content. Their combined efforts will help establish the organization's footing in the competitive scene.

---

## Expanding the Digital Footprint

Alongside the roster acquisition, kt Rolster announced a restructuring of its digital presence. The organization is shifting its existing FC Online channel into a comprehensive hub called kt Rolster_esports. This consolidated platform will host content for the newly formed Street Fighter squad alongside other competitive divisions.
