The anticipation is building for **Capcom Cup 12**, set to take place from March 11 through March 15 at the historic Ryogoku Kokugikan in Tokyo, Japan. With a life-changing $1,000,000 grand prize awaiting the victor, the bracket is primed for the highest level of Street Fighter 6 competition.

**Key Storylines to Watch:**
* **The Empty Throne:** Capcom Cup 11 champion Kakeru has officially retired. His absence leaves the ultimate crown completely up for grabs.
* **MenaRD's Dynasty:** The Dominican powerhouse is already the only two-time Capcom Cup champion in history. He is hunting for an unprecedented third title.
* **Teenage Redemption:** Sixteen-year-old Chilean prodigy Blaz stormed to second place last year and returns to finish the job.

---

## The Stakes and the Gauntlet: A Grueling Tokyo Format

Returning to the legendary Ryogoku Kokugikan adds massive prestige to this year's finale. The Tokyo venue is the spiritual home of sumo wrestling. The stakes have never been higher for the 48 qualified players. The Capcom Pro Tour 2025 circuit boasts a massive overall prize pool, but all eyes are heavily focused on the $1,000,000 reserved strictly for the Capcom Cup 12 champion. 

To claim that prize, players must survive one of the most grueling tournament formats in fighting game history. The 48 competitors are divided into 12 groups of four for Phase 1. They will play a double-elimination bracket where all matches are First-to-3. The player who wins the upper bracket in each group advances directly to the Top 16 finals. The remaining survivors fall into Phase 2, entering four groups of three for a brutal round-robin stage. Only the top player from each Phase 2 group escapes into the finals. Once the Top 16 is set, the format shifts to a terrifying single-elimination bracket. The finals become an exhausting First-to-5 marathon to crown a champion. 

---

## The Heavyweights: Chasing Street Fighter Supremacy

MenaRD stands as the undisputed favorite heading into Tokyo. His dominant 2025 season includes massive victories at EVO Japan, EVO Vegas, and SFL Pro-US. Armed with his signature Blanka, his aggressive pressure and championship pedigree make him the ultimate final boss of the event. He is joined at the top of the rankings by Korea's LeShar. LeShar claimed three CPT Premier wins this season and enters the tournament as a surgical precision machine wielding Ed and Terry.

Secondary contenders bring equally terrifying recent results to the main stage. Japan's Fuudo brings his analytical mastery and heavy tank playstyle to his third straight Capcom Cup appearance. After suffering back-to-back ninth-place finishes, the Blink Respawn champion is determined to break his curse and push into the Top 8. Korea's NL continues to melt Drive Gauges with Akuma. He secured a massive victory at CEO 2025 earlier this year. Both veterans have the experience required to navigate the high-pressure group stages.

---

## The Bracket Disruptors: Dark Horses to Watch

The field is packed with rising stars ready to cause massive bracket upsets. The most compelling narrative belongs to Blaz. At just 15 years old, he shocked the world by taking second place at Capcom Cup 11. Now 16, he has refined his Ryu and Sagat play. He earned runner-up finishes at major events leading up to Tokyo. Similarly, 17-year-old Hong Kong native Micky forced the community to pay attention with an incredible 12-match losers bracket streak at EVO Vegas to finish in third place.

The international regions also bring unique challengers who could easily dismantle the favorites. Britain's EndingWalker remained completely undefeated in the SFL Pro-Europe regular season with his Ryu. He constantly drags opponents into unfamiliar territory. The retirement of defending champion Kakeru removes a massive obstacle from the bracket. This clears a direct path for these hungry dark horses to claim the million-dollar prize.
