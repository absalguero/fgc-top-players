DetonatioN FocusMe has acquired multi-game veteran KEI.B from Nagoya OJA following his 9-1 run in the 2025 Street Fighter League season.

<div class="takeaways">

**Key Takeaways:**
* **KEI.B departs Nagoya OJA after a 2025 SFL performance where he secured nine wins in ten matches.**
* **The transfer reunites him with long-time friend and fellow competitor GO1.**
* **The legacy Japanese organization continues to expand its footprint in the fighting game ecosystem.**

</div>

## SFL Dominance and Roster Transfer

The transfer pulls one of Japan's top multi-game talents away from Nagoya OJA. KEI.B competed under his former organization's banner for three consecutive years before making this transition. His 2025 campaign proved essential to their playoff qualification. He secured a 9-1 record during the preliminary stage to top the overall league standings. 

> **"I will contribute to the team utilizing the experience I've cultivated so far, and this year I will actively participate in overseas tournaments aiming for EWC and CC."**
> — *KEI.B, DetonatioN FocusMe Competitor*

---

## A Decade of Japanese Esports Legacy

DetonatioN FocusMe operates as one of the oldest and most established esports organizations in Japan. Nobuyuki Umezaki founded the group in July 2012 to compete in Counter-Strike. The organization later found its greatest international success through the League of Legends ecosystem. That specific squad made history in 2021 by becoming the first Japanese team to reach the main stage of the World Championship. 

The parent company GameWith merged its own esports division with DetonatioN in late 2022. This integration consolidated all competitive rosters worldwide under the DetonatioN FocusMe banner. The group has maintained a steady presence in the fighting game space over the years by sponsoring top-tier Street Fighter talent including Itabashi Zangief, Nauman, and John Takeuchi.

---

## Reuniting with GO1

This roster acquisition builds upon existing relationships within the DFM camp. The newest signing and GO1 have maintained a close friendship for years, with both competitors even listing each other as close friends in their official SFL profiles. DFM management expects this personal bond to translate into immediate collaborative strength for team events. 

The organization is actively targeting the Japanese SFL championship with this updated roster. KEI.B outlined his individual competitive goals immediately upon signing. He plans to travel for overseas tournaments rather than focusing strictly on domestic leagues, specifically targeting qualification spots for the Esports World Cup and Capcom Cup.
