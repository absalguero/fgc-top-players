The latest Street Fighter 6 patch did almost nothing to disrupt the established top tier according to former Capcom Cup champion Derek "iDom" Ruffin. 

<div class="takeaways">

**Key Takeaways:**
* **iDom believes the top five characters remain unchallenged, though performance data suggests JP has slipped to A Tier.**

## The Theory vs Data Disconnect

Ed, Blanka, JP, Sagat, and Mai continue to dominate the conversation surrounding tournament brackets. iDom broke down the mid-season state of the game in a new video upload. He echoed sentiments from the Japanese competitive scene that the best characters remain mostly untouched by the developers. 

This perception directly clashes with current performance metrics. iDom places JP firmly in the top five. Both the [SmashlogTV Japanese panel](https://fgctopplayers.com/news/smashlogtv-ranks-the-street-fighter-6-roster-for-patch-317/) and the FGC Top Players data index agree that JP currently operates at a lower A Tier level. The gap between what players feel during casual sets and the reality of rated brackets is widening. 

> **"Viper actually got a buff in the neutral department cuz burn kick's just kind of obnoxious right now."**
> — *Derek "iDom" Ruffin, Pro Player*

---

## The Viper Illusion and Shoto Slog

Crimson Viper serves as the perfect example of this theorycrafting divide. Her burn kick modifications granted her an impressive positional advantage on paper. SmashlogTV panelists John Takeuchi and Moke promoted her straight to S Tier based on these frame data changes. iDom similarly praised her new utility. The raw tournament data tells a separate story. The FGC Top Players stats leaderboard currently traps C. Viper down in C Tier.

The traditional shoto archetype faces a similarly confusing landscape. iDom groups Terry, Ryu, and Akuma together as functionally identical in the current meta. Ryu lost his solar plexus strike and inflicts less drive gauge chip damage. These fighters now compete for the exact same narrow niche.

## The Shifting Post-Patch Landscape

Grappler players have virtually nothing to celebrate in these subjective tier lists. Zangief, Manon, and Lily remain trapped at the bottom of iDom's rankings. Their core gameplans rely on one-dimensional strategies that top tiers dismantle with ease. 

The 3.17 update cycle is still in its infancy. Competitors need time to adapt their bracket strategies to the adjusted mechanics. Crimson Viper possesses clear theoretical advantages. She has plenty of time to rise up the data rankings if players start turning theory into strong tournament results. Zangief faces the opposite trajectory. His high global win rates could plummet as opponents learn to exploit his latest adjustments. Upcoming events will reveal whether bracket statistics hold firm or if the theoretical predictions become reality.
