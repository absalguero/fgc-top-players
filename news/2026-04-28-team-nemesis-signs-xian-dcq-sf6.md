Evo champion Xian and Chinese powerhouse DCQ have found a new organizational home. Team Nemesis secured the two international veterans to launch its Street Fighter 6 division. Formed by the former leadership of Into The Breach, the expanding organization announced the acquisition on social media to clarify its commitment to the fighting game space. This roster addition provides strong backing for the decorated players while cementing the club's broader ambitions. Team Nemesis secured a spot among the Top 100 Esports Clubs at the upcoming Esports World Cup.

## Club Championship Strategy

Signing top-tier fighting game talent is a calculated strategy for organizations aiming to secure points in the EWC Club Championship. Fielding established threats like Xian and DCQ positions Team Nemesis to aggressively compete for a larger share of the cross-title prize pool. The organization operates competitive rosters in Dota 2, CS2, and Valorant. Expanding into Capcom's flagship title diversifies their competitive portfolio heading into the summer event schedule.

---

## Championship Pedigree

Both players bring deep tournament experience to the organization. Xian won Evo in 2013 and maintains a reputation for sharp execution and deep character knowledge. DCQ consistently represents the pinnacle of Chinese talent with dominant bracket runs at major international events. Their combined expertise gives Nemesis an immediate and credible presence in the highest tiers of competitive play. 

---

## Global Tournament Circuit

With the Esports World Cup and the Capcom Pro Tour driving the competitive calendar, Team Nemesis is positioned to make an immediate impact. Xian and DCQ now possess the institutional backing to travel extensively and focus on accumulating global ranking points. Their performance in upcoming international brackets will test the effectiveness of the organization's expansion strategy.
