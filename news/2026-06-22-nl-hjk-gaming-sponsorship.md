South Korean Street Fighter 6 veteran NL has secured an official device partnership with HJK Gaming to support his competitive campaign throughout the remainder of the 2026 circuit.

<div class="takeaways">

**Key Takeaways:**
* **Official Partnership:** Pro player NL is now officially supported by peripheral manufacturer HJK Gaming.
* **Hardware Focused:** The deal provides NL with high-performance leverless controller hardware for critical international events.
* **Eyes on Riyadh:** The partnership specifically targets support for upcoming runs at BAM 16 and the EWC 2026 Last Chance Qualifier.

</div>

---

## Hardware Backing for the Competitive Grind

HJK Gaming is a specialized manufacturer emerging as a fixture in the fighting game peripheral scene, best known for their "Hitpad" leverless controllers. Designed with direct input from high-level competitors, their hardware focuses on low-latency performance, durable PBT keycaps, and ergonomic layouts intended for tournament play. By aligning with a brand that focuses heavily on the mechanics of the "box" controller, NL gains access to specialized equipment designed to handle the high-input demands of modern Street Fighter 6.

This device partnership provides NL with consistent technical support as he prepares to travel for upcoming major tournaments. In the current FGC landscape, where peripheral latency and button reliability are paramount, having a dedicated manufacturer-level partnership ensures that players can maintain setup consistency regardless of their location.

---

## The Road to the Esports World Cup

The partnership arrives at a decisive moment in the season. NL is currently gearing up for two major international stops. He is set to compete at BAM 16, using the event as a critical testing ground for his current bracket execution. Following that, he will turn his attention to the Esports World Cup 2026 Last Chance Qualifier.

Securing an invite through an LCQ is notoriously difficult, requiring a high level of endurance and mental fortitude. With the backing of HJK Gaming, NL aims to minimize hardware friction, allowing him to focus entirely on the volatile, high-stakes brackets that define the road to Riyadh. This move suggests a broader trend of players seeking specific, high-performance peripheral sponsors to gain an edge in the increasingly competitive global meta.
