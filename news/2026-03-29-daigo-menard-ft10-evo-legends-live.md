A first-to-10 format completely changes the game by stripping away bracket volatility and forcing players to adapt over potentially nineteen matches. When you put two of the most decorated competitors in Street Fighter history on that stage, you get an absolute war. Daigo Umehara and MenaRD are gearing up to do exactly that at Evo Legends Live on April 29, 2026.

## The Beast Path

Kemonomichi basically translates to "The Beast Path" in Japanese. Daigo built this exhibition series specifically to remove the chaos of standard bracket runs. Short sets often reward volatile strategies and lucky guesses. Long sets demand pure mental endurance. The rules are brutal but simple. The first player to win ten games takes the entire set. Players cannot rely on a lower bracket run to save them if they start slow. 

Evo organizers dropped the match details during the recent awards presentation. Fans are getting a perfect clash of contrasting styles and generations. Daigo holds an unmatched legacy built over decades of international competition. The Japanese veteran relies on calculated spacing and absolute fundamental precision to slowly dismantle opponents. He created this format to test players in an environment where adaptation is the only way to survive.

---

## A Clash of Generations

MenaRD brings a completely different energy to the stage. The competitor from the Dominican Republic holds two Capcom Cup championships. Opponents often fold under his relentless conditioning and aggressive pressure. He earned the nickname The Bull by steamrolling the competition. Competing against a seasoned veteran in a long set will test his ability to pace his explosive offense.

Standard tournaments do not prepare players for this kind of grind. Both competitors have to evolve their strategies mid-match. MenaRD has raw momentum and explosive offense on his side. Daigo has decades of experience and the home-field advantage of a format he designed. The upcoming showdown promises to push both players to their absolute limits.
