**Sycom** has officially entered the competitive arena, confirming a major shift from brand sponsorship to direct involvement in the fighting game community.

**Key Takeaways:**
* **Sycom Transitions to Frontline:** The Build-To-Order (BTO) PC manufacturer moves from behind-the-scenes sponsorships to fielding its own official esports roster.
* **Street Fighter 6 Division Established:** The company has signed a dedicated roster of four players to compete in Capcom's flagship fighting game.
* **SFL Speculation:** The timely signing of a four-man roster positions the brand as a potential candidate to fill the vacant Street Fighter League spot left by CAG Osaka.

---

## Sycom Enters the Arena as Official Competitor

Sycom is a prominent Build-To-Order (BTO) PC manufacturer. The brand officially launched its own in-house esports team recently. The company has historically acted as a behind-the-scenes pillar for the competitive gaming community by sponsoring numerous professional teams and influencers. 

With the esports market continuing to mature, Sycom recognized a need to evolve its strategy. Instead of remaining solely as an external sponsor, the hardware manufacturer is stepping onto the frontline. The organization expressed a strong desire to stand alongside players as active participants. The new project was launched with the clear intention of cultivating excitement and compelling storylines directly from the competitive trenches.

---

## Official Street Fighter 6 Roster Revealed

The brand is making its initial foray into active competition by establishing a division dedicated exclusively to Street Fighter 6. Sycom has officially signed Japanese competitors Orarin, Zabuton, Pipokun, and Imai Shota to represent the brand. The new Sycom esports project is built on the philosophy of shifting from passive support to active co-creation. 

The organization emphasized that its goals extend far beyond tournament placements and win-loss records. Sycom intends to share the profound process of personal and professional growth that players experience. The company hopes this approach will elevate the overall passion and energy within the gaming scene. The newly acquired roster brings significant competitive experience to the new organization. Zabuton is widely known for his elite Dee Jay gameplay. He secured first place at the SCARZ Genesis Tournament in 2025 and reached the Master Rate world number one rank during the 10th competitive ranked season. 

---

## Evaluating the CAG Osaka SFL Vacancy

Direct involvement from major hardware manufacturers provides crucial infrastructure for these aspiring professionals as the competitive gaming ecosystem continues to expand. This news arrives shortly after CAG Osaka announced the suspension of its fighting game division and its formal withdrawal from Street Fighter League 2026. This sudden departure has left a highly coveted franchise spot open in Japan's premier fighting game circuit.

Fans and analysts have naturally begun speculating about which organization might step up to fill the void. Sycom's entry into the competitive sphere proves they have the financial backing to support a high-level team. Furthermore, their newly signed roster features exactly four skilled Street Fighter 6 competitors. This headcount aligns perfectly with the standard four-player team format required for the Street Fighter League. While no official announcements have been made regarding the SFL vacancy, Sycom has positioned itself as a highly logical candidate to acquire the open slot.
