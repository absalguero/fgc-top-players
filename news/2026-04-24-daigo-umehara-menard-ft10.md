In a documentary feature from Daigo the BeasTV, veteran Japanese fighting game competitors are openly bracing for the possibility that Daigo Umehara might fall to MenaRD in their upcoming First-to-10 exhibition.

<div class="takeaways">

**Key Takeaways:**

* **MenaRD's past back-to-back major victories frame the immense challenge facing the veteran in the eyes of his peers.**
* **Competitors emphasize Daigo's terrifying ability to adapt in First-to-10 formats to counter his opponent's momentum.**

</div>

---

## Reflecting on a Legacy

The video compilation highlights deeply personal interviews with peers who credit Daigo with establishing the pro gaming ecosystem. These players express a palpable mix of anxiety and reverence ahead of the [Evo Legends Live exhibition scheduled for April 29, 2026](https://fgctopplayers.com/news/daigo-and-menard-prepare-for-grueling-ft10-at-evo-legends-live/). While standard tournament brackets test immediate execution over short bursts, several players in the feature share anecdotes of being completely dismantled by Daigo in extended formats. A First-to-10 set demands a drastically different approach to neutral interactions. The interviewees note Daigo excels when given the time to download an opponent over multiple games.

---

## The FGPI Mountain to Climb

The interviews position MenaRD as an overwhelming force. The specter of his dominant tournament runs heavily influences how the Japanese scene views this matchup. Examining the current Fighting Game Performance Index (FGPI), Daigo faces an opponent built on relentless offensive pressure. The raw data favors the younger player heavily. The interviewed pros readily admit this statistical reality while hoping the long-set format levels the playing field.

---

## A Generational Clash

The anxiety expressed throughout the video stems from what this match represents for the older generation of competitors. A loss for Daigo against the game's most dominant modern force could symbolize a changing of the guard at the absolute highest level of play. Despite these fears, the overarching sentiment from the featured players remains a unified desire to see Daigo win. They want the veteran to prove the doubters wrong and showcase his trademark First-to-10 resilience.
