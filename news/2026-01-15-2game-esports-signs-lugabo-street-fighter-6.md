**Lugabo** has officially joined **2Game Esports** on Thursday, confirming that the organization has secured one of North America's brightest young talents ahead of Capcom Cup 12.

**Key Takeaways:**
* **Quadruple Threat:** With Lugabo's signing, 2Game Esports now boasts **four** players qualified for Capcom Cup 12: Lugabo, Shaka, Blaz, and Tashi.
* **Youth Movement:** At just 16 years old, Lugabo represents the "new generation" of *Street Fighter 6* talent.
* **LatAm Superteam:** The roster now unifies top talent from Mexico, Argentina, Chile, and Venezuela alongside its Brazilian core.

---

## Lugabo Joins 2Game Esports

2Game Esports announced the acquisition of **Lugabo** on Thursday evening. The signing brings the 16-year-old phenom into the fold just weeks before he is set to compete at Capcom Cup 12. Lugabo dominated the highly competitive Mexico region during the 2025 season, culminating in a victory at the World Warrior Regional Final that punched his ticket to the season's grand finale.

The organization highlighted Lugabo's youth and potential in their official statement, noting that he "arrives to add up" to their growing legacy. By securing a fourth Capcom Cup qualified player, 2Game has positioned itself as perhaps the best-represented organization at the upcoming world championship.

---

## A Continental Powerhouse

The addition of Lugabo solidifies 2Game Esports as the premier destination for Latin American talent. The organization has effectively built a continental superteam that bridges the Portuguese and Spanish-speaking communities of the Americas.

Lugabo (Mexico) joins a formidable lineup of qualified teammates:
* **Blaz** (Chile): The veteran competitor who has become a staple of the global scene.
* **Shaka** (Argentina): The Dhalsim specialist who recently joined the squad.
* **Tashi** (Venezuela): Another rising star who secured his qualification via the Central America West region.

This recruitment strategy balances veteran experience with explosive new talent, ensuring 2Game has multiple viable paths to the title while representing the full breadth of the Latin American scene.
