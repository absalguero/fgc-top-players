With the 2025 regular season officially concluded, the stage is set for the **Tekken World Tour Finals 2025**. The complete roster of elite players is locked in and preparing to compete for the world championship title next month in Malmö, Sweden.

**Key Takeaways:**
* **Mulgold** secures the #1 Global Seed after a dominant season.
* **Arslan Ash, Knee, and ULSAN** headline a stacked bracket of returning legends and top contenders.
* **Malmö, Sweden** hosts the Global Finals from January 29 to February 1, 2026.

---

## The Road to Malmö: Roster Locked

The gruelling 2025 season has concluded, and the field for the **Tekken World Tour Finals 2025** is now set. Following the final Master event at Thaiger Uppercut in late November, Bandai Namco has locked in the top 20 players from the Global Leaderboard and the 15 champions from the Regional Leaderboards. These competitors will converge in Malmö, Sweden, early next year to determine the undisputed King of the Iron Fist.

South Korea's **Mulgold** finished atop the Global Leaderboard, earning the prestigious first seed. He is joined in the top ranks by Pakistan's **Arslan Ash** and **ATIF**, cementing Pakistan's continued dominance in the *Tekken 8* meta.

The roster also features FGC titans such as **Knee**, **ULSAN**, and **Nobi**, ensuring a clash of generations and styles. While the global heavyweights secured their spots through consistent performances at Master and Challenger events, the Regional Leaderboard winners fought through localized gauntlets to earn their tickets to Sweden.

---

## Event Format & Prize Details

The **Tekken World Tour Finals 2025** will utilize a hybrid format designed to test consistency and clutch factor.

* **Group Stage (Jan 29-30):** The bottom 16 global qualifiers will be split into four groups on Day 1, while the 15 regional qualifiers will form three separate groups on Day 2.
* **Advancement:** Top performers from these groups will advance to the main bracket to meet the high seeds.
* **Last Chance Qualifier (Jan 31):** The LCQ is an open tournament for any player who has not already qualified for the Finals. The winner of this bracket will claim the single final spot in the Top 20 Finals.

The main event concludes with the Top 20 Finals on February 1, 2026.

## What’s Next

The **Tekken World Tour Finals 2025** officially kicks off on **January 29, 2026**, with the Global Leaderboard Group Stages, followed by the Regional Leaderboard Group Stages on January 30.

Once the initial phases whittle down the qualified field, the focus will shift to the **Last Chance Qualifier** (LCQ) on January 31. Unqualified competitors from around the world will descend on Malmö for a brutal open bracket to claim the sole remaining slot in the final Top 20 bracket on February 1.
