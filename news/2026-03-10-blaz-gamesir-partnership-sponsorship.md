**The Blaz-GameSir partnership** has officially been announced, confirming the 16-year-old Chilean Street Fighter 6 competitor as the new Global Brand Ambassador for the peripheral manufacturer. 

**Key Takeaways:**
* **Blaz will represent GameSir on the world stage at the upcoming Capcom Cup 12 in Tokyo.**
* **GameSir continues its deep push into the competitive fighting game ecosystem with new top-tier talent and hardware.**
* **The young competitor adds this major backing to an already massive resume following his breakout 2025 season.**

---

## The Ascent of a Chilean Prodigy

Derek Blaz has quickly made a lasting impact on the competitive Street Fighter 6 scene. The 16-year-old competitor has transitioned from a rising regional talent to an apex predator in the global fighting game world. His historic bracket runs have caught the attention of major hardware brands looking to back players who demand absolute perfection in their inputs. Blaz already represents the 2GAME esports organization and will now add GameSir to his list of major backers.

This sponsorship follows an incredible 2025 season for the young competitor. He secured massive second-place finishes at both Capcom Cup 11 and the Esports World Cup. These deep bracket runs solidified his reputation as a top-tier threat. Fighting game competitors require reliable inputs during high-stress tournament situations, and this deal ensures Blaz is fully equipped with pro-grade gear as he continues his competitive journey.

---

## Equipping for the Tokyo Gauntlet

Founded in 2013 by Yao Ma in Guangzhou, China, GameSir has built a strong reputation for producing high-quality gaming peripherals. The company is well-regarded for its cost-effective approach and technological innovations. They are known for anti-stick drift capabilities and high-performance esports controllers. GameSir is aggressively targeting the fighting game community with new hardware announcements like the newly revealed Delta-X9 fight stick.

The timing of this specific partnership aligns perfectly with the culmination of the competitive Street Fighter season. Blaz is actively preparing for his highly anticipated appearance at Capcom Cup 12 in Tokyo, which is running until the 15th. Competing for the largest prize pool in the circuit requires absolute confidence in your peripherals. This new backing provides the young competitor with essential hardware support as he attempts to redefine the new era of fighting games.
