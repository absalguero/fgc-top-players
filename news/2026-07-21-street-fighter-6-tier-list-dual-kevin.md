With approximately one month remaining before Capcom delivers its next balance patch, professional Rashid player Dual Kevin crowned Mai as the best character in Street Fighter 6. He evaluated the full roster to outline where every fighter stands in the current meta. His final Season 3 tier list highlights a sharp shift away from early-game standouts.

<div class="takeaways">

**Key Takeaways:**
* **Mai claims the number one spot in Dual Kevin's Season 3 tier list thanks to her dominant fan pressure, invincible Level 1 stock super, and neutral control.**
* **Sagat and Blanka round out the top three in S-tier, joining Ed, JP, and Terry as the core powerhouses of the competitive meta.**
* **FGC Top Players tournament analytics mirror his top placement for Mai, though empirical data indicates fighters like Ken struggle more in bracket than pro sentiment suggests.**

</div>

---

## Mai Leads S-Tier Domination

Despite receiving minor balance adjustments in prior updates, Mai continues to set the standard for top-level play. Dual Kevin highlights her EX fan sequences, dominant frame pressure, and high-reward Level 2 super setups as key reasons for her supremacy. Her stocked Level 1 super gives her arguably the strongest defensive tool in the game.

> **"I think Ma's the best character in the game even after the slight nerfs that she got."**
> — *Dual Kevin, Professional Street Fighter 6 Player*

Sagat follows Mai closely at number two due to his straightforward execution and high damage output from heavy button starters. He noted that Sagat's Tiger Nexus mind games force opponents to expend drive meter constantly. Blanka takes third behind Sagat, bolstered by his fast neutral options and unpunishable Drive Rush retreats. Ed and JP round out the top five with their screen-wide zoning control and drive-depleting blockstrings.

## Meta Shifts and Tournament Data Alignment

The tier list highlights how much the competitive scene evolved throughout Season 3. Heavy hitters that dominated the end of Season 2 and early Season 3, including Akuma, M. Bison, and Rashid, now sit just outside S-tier. Late Season 2 saw Akuma overpower brackets with charged fireball setups and glass-cannon damage, while M. Bison dictated neutral through oppressive scissor kick frame traps and psycho crusher projectile invincibility.

While those fighters remain potent in A-tier, Dual Kevin places Mai, Sagat, and Blanka ahead of them due to their superior defensive consistency and easier drive-depletion loops. Akuma and M. Bison still dish out explosive damage, but defensive vulnerabilities and reliance on specific setups prevent them from claiming top-three status in the current climate.

Dual Kevin's read on the top of the meta aligns closely with empirical tournament data. The FGC Top Players CPV tier list ranks Mai as the top character based on 12 months of rated tournament results, while supporting Sagat and Blanka as top-three threats. Tournament analytics show a sharper decline for mid-tier fighters, as the CPV index places Ken in D-tier due to reduced bracket conversion rates at major events.

## Strategic Takeaways for Capcom's Next Update

Dual Kevin's evaluation offers a complete snapshot of a mature Season 3 meta as players prepare for Capcom's next major balance update. With characters like Mai, Sagat, and Blanka dictating tournament brackets, the upcoming patch will determine whether Capcom chooses to tone down top-tier fan setups and zoning options or elevate struggling mid-tier options.

For competitors heading into upcoming major events, understanding these tier dynamics remains essential for bracket preparation. As the meta continues to evolve, player sentiment and empirical tournament results will remain key indicators of character strength heading into Season 4.
