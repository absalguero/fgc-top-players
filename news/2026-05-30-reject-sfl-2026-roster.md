## REJECT's 2026 Roster Reveal

Team REJECT has officially unveiled their roster for the 2026 Street Fighter League season, leaning into a mix of legendary experience and rising talent. Daigo Umehara takes the reins as team leader, a role he acknowledged with a mix of pragmatism and visible motivation. He made it clear he is taking the responsibility seriously, focusing heavily on team dynamics and preparation rather than just resting on past laurels.

The most compelling narrative of the broadcast centered on Jitsuchoku (Yasu). Previously known primarily as a streamer, Jitsuchoku earned his spot on the SFL stage through sheer performance. The team revealed that he was dominating in REJECT's private sparring sessions, proving his competitive viability against seasoned professionals. His transition from content creator to league competitor adds a significant wildcard element to REJECT's lineup. 

Rounding out the roster are Fuudo, who emphasized the importance of offline practice and early preparation, and young phenom Hikaru, sporting the "Ace Number" 10 jersey. The dynamic between the veterans and the newer players, specifically the 30-year age gap between the oldest and youngest, was a recurring theme, with the team viewing it as a strategic advantage rather than a hurdle.

---

## Early Meta Reads: Testing Ingrid

The broadcast also provided a glimpse into the team's early character strategies, particularly concerning the newly added DLC character, Ingrid. While acknowledging her strength, the players were quick to point out her steep learning curve and significant mechanical flaws.

The team noted that Ingrid is highly technical, requiring precise "stock" management to execute her optimal air combos and stun setups following her beam attack. More critically, they highlighted a major defensive vulnerability: an 8-frame startup on her defensive options. This makes her highly susceptible to safe jumps, a flaw that top-level players will undoubtedly exploit. 

While Fuudo confirmed he is primarily sticking with Ed and testing C. Viper, and Yasu committed to Ryu despite current meta challenges, the team's willingness to lab new tools suggests a flexible approach to the upcoming season.

---

## Preparing for the 2026 Season

With the season kickoff approaching, REJECT's strategy seems heavily focused on building early momentum through intensive, in-person practice. Fuudo’s emphasis on offline sessions indicates the team is prioritizing the kind of tight communication and rapid adaptation that defined previous championship runs. 

The combination of Daigo's leadership, Fuudo's strategic grounding, Hikaru's explosive potential, and Jitsuchoku's raw drive creates a volatile but highly dangerous roster. As the meta continues to shift with new character additions, REJECT's blend of generational talent will be put to the test.
