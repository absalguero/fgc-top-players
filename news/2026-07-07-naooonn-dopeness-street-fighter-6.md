Following a spectacular top-eight performance at Evo Japan, breakout Akuma specialist Naooonn has secured a sponsorship deal with esports organization DOPENESS. The Japanese organization finalized the agreement to give the rising star an immediate path to international travel, transitioning him from regional tournaments to the global circuit.

**Key Takeaways:**
* **Evo Japan breakout star Naooonn joins DOPENESS** after an impressive top-eight placement in the 7,000-entrant tournament.
* **DOPENESS enters the fighting game community** for the first time, expanding outside of its existing PUBG Mobile and eFootball divisions.
* **International travel becomes the immediate focus** as the newly sponsored Akuma player prepares for the Esports World Cup Last Chance Qualifier.

---

## Evolution of an Akuma Specialist

Naooonn captured the attention of the fighting game community during Evo Japan, which set an all-time record with over 7,000 entrants. His aggressive playstyle dismantled top contenders. While active since the Street Fighter V era, he lacked a dedicated team to fund international travel. DOPENESS aims to change that dynamic. Joining a stable organization provides the structural support required to maintain a full-time competitive regimen.

---

## DOPENESS Expands into Fighting Games

The signing represents a clear strategic expansion for DOPENESS. The brand previously concentrated its competitive efforts on mobile and sports titles, specifically fielding rosters in PUBG Mobile and eFootball. By acquiring an elite Street Fighter 6 competitor, the organization establishes an immediate presence in the fighting game community. This transition comes at a time when fighting games are drawing peak viewership numbers globally. Naooonn will anchor the newly formed division as its premier player, setting a foundation for potential future signings.

---

## Preparing for the Last Chance Qualifier

With organizational backing, the player can now shift his focus to the global tournament circuit. He has competed primarily within Japan up to this point. The immediate priority under his new banner is the upcoming Esports World Cup Last Chance Qualifier. This tournament gives players a final opportunity to secure a highly coveted spot on the main stage in Paris. International brackets will have to adapt quickly to Naooonn and his lethal Akuma. High-level execution on the world stage will determine if this investment pays off for the brand.
