Nagoya NTPOJA is building an entire competitive squad around local hometown heroes for the upcoming season. The organization revealed its selection, emphasizing a homegrown lineup designed to bring regional talent to the global stage.

<div class="takeaways">

**Key Takeaways:**
* **Local Focus:** The entire active lineup and support staff share direct geographic ties to the Nagoya region.
* **The 2026 Core:** Seiya, Raoyamu, Otani, and Mocchy comprise the four-player competitive roster.
* **Support Structure:** Analyst popoLinLin and support captain Makky complete the staff to assist with data management and fan engagement.

</div>

---

## Meet the All-Nagoya Lineup

To understand why this roster is turning heads, you have to look at the individual journeys of these four competitors. The squad features a calculated mix of tactical specialists, legacy veterans, and crossover champions who all share regional roots.

* **Seiya:** Known within the competitive community as a defensive mastermind. He specializes in playing Chun-Li, a character requiring extreme spatial awareness and precision. Seiya neutralizes highly aggressive opponents through immense patience and flawless counter-attacks.
* **Raoyamu:** A rising star who recently left the Indomitable Panda organization to join his home team. He relies on heavy pressure and relentless offense, utilizing characters like M. Bison and Marisa to bully opponents into making mistakes.
* **Otani:** A seasoned competitive veteran with a deep history in the fighting game scene. He remains a consistent fixture at the highest ranks of online play, piloting characters like Ken and Terry with high-speed control and optimal combo damage.
* **Mocchy:** A legendary competitor from rival fighting game franchises. He secured multiple world championship titles in games like Guilty Gear Strive before transitioning his elite mechanical skills full-time to Street Fighter 6, where he now commands Akuma.

---

## Nagoya NTPOJA 2026 Complete Roster

The staff structure aims to support the four active competitors through a grueling league format. Analyst popoLinLin will manage data and match preparation, a crucial role in a league where character counter-picks dictate success. Support captain Makky will lead community engagement efforts.

## Regional Narrative in the Pro-JP League

This localized approach will face its ultimate test when the competitive season begins. Maintaining a single-region roster limits the recruitment pool but creates a distinct narrative in an increasingly corporate esports landscape. Success here could spark a new trend of localized team building across other Japanese esports organizations.
