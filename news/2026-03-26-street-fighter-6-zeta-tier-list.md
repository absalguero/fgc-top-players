The post-patch Street Fighter 6 meta is stabilizing, and ZETA DIVISION's latest broadcast positions C.Viper as a breakout top-tier threat. 

<div class="takeaways">

**Key Takeaways:**
* **C.Viper jumps in viability due to favorable frame data on her standing heavy punch.**
* **Sagat remains a dominant force at the top of the rankings with excellent space control.**
* **Ed retains a high placement but faces skepticism about his long-term power after system tweaks.**

</div>

---

## Sagat Solidifies Top Tier Status

ZETA DIVISION members Momochi, Higuchi, and Yamaguchi broadcasted their tier list discussion. The trio agreed the current character balance feels remarkably flat compared to previous seasons. Sagat emerged as a primary candidate for the strongest character in the game. His baseline kit remains largely untouched. He maintains oppressive space control with his standing normals, keeping most of the cast at a severe disadvantage. 

The players noted Ed's overall pressure feels less overwhelming now. The gap between him and the rest of the roster has closed. Some players feel his reverse-sweep potential has dropped. He still secures a spot in the highest tier, but his absolute dominance is now up for debate.

---

## C.Viper's Explosive Potential

C.Viper generated the most debate during the broadcast. The team noted her standing heavy punch is now advantageous on block. This single adjustment forces opponents to respect her offense much more than in previous versions. One player suggested she has the potential to be the absolute best character in the game. Her overall game plan is simpler to execute now, allowing players to press their advantage without relying on risky workarounds.

## Shifts in the Mid-Tier

The lower end of the roster saw distinct downward shifts. Characters like Ken and Luke are slipping down the rankings. The professionals feel there is less reason to pick them when characters like Ryu offer similar utility with better overall damage. Dee Jay received slight offensive improvements to his crouch medium punch strings but suffers defensively. His crouch heavy kick changes make him far more susceptible to whiff punishes.
