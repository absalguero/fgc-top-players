Capcom Cup champion Sahara placed Sagat and Mai at the absolute top of his Patch 3.17 tier list while isolating Alex in a custom bottom tier.

<div class="takeaways">

**Key Takeaways:**
* **Sahara keeps JP in the top five to align with competitive evaluations from iDom.**
* **Crimson Viper lands in A Tier and misses out on the S Tier hype generated by early patch analysis.**
* **Alex sits completely alone at the bottom in a custom lyric-based C Tier.**

## The Meta Clash

The reigning world champion shared his latest impressions during a broadcast of Kachitagari TV. Sahara positioned Ed, JP, and Rashid alongside Sagat and Mai to round out a formidable S Tier. This top-five evaluation aligns closely with [competitive analysis from former champion iDom](https://fgctopplayers.com/news/idom-breaks-down-sf6-mid-season-meta-theory-clashes-with-bracket-data/). Both players view JP as a premier threat. This directly contradicts the Japanese panel at [SmashlogTV](https://fgctopplayers.com/news/smashlogtv-ranks-the-street-fighter-6-roster-for-patch-317/). Those analysts dropped JP to A Tier based on theoretical frame data adjustments.

The A Tier placements reveal deep divides regarding character strength. Sahara placed Crimson Viper in a crowded eleven-character bracket. SmashlogTV panelists promoted her straight to S Tier following her burning kick buffs. Data from FGC Top Players shows that this Viper speculation clashes heavily with actual bracket results. Sahara's assessment seems to find a middle ground between hard statistics and training mode potential.

---

## Sahara's Complete Patch 3.17 Placements

Sahara sorted the rest of the cast into the lower ranks. The solitary C Tier placement for Alex includes a playful nod to his character theme lyrics. Sahara labeled this bottom rank with a Japanese phrase translating roughly to "Fighting with burning fighting spirit, bet on this ring, Fighter Alex." SmashlogTV viewed the grappler much more favorably with a solid B Tier ranking.
