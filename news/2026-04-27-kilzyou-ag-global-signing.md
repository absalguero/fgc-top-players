AG Global continues its expansion into the fighting game community by signing French *Street Fighter 6* standout Kilzyou. The roster move secures vital tournament representation for the organization at the upcoming Esports World Cup.

<div class="takeaways">

**Key Takeaways:**
* **AG Global adds Kilzyou to compete in the highly contested Street Fighter 6 bracket.**
* **The roster move directly supports the organization's push for the EWC Club Championship.**
* **Kilzyou joins an expanding fighting game division that already includes SF6 competitors Vxbao and Zhen.**

</div>

---

## Chasing the Club Championship

The Esports World Cup features a lucrative overarching prize pool that rewards organizations for placing high across multiple different gaming titles. Organizations cannot rely on a single dominant team to take home the club trophy. They must cast a wide net across the entire event lineup to secure tournament placements. Earning top-eight finishes yields the crucial points needed to climb the final organizational rankings.

The fighting game community offers a dense concentration of distinct titles at the Riyadh event. This unique structure makes top FGC players highly valuable to tier-one esports teams. Adding a player with Kilzyou's consistency ensures AG Global maintains a competitive presence in the massive *Street Fighter 6* bracket. His precise fundamentals make him a consistent threat to advance deep into the tournament and secure those necessary placements.

---

## Consolidating Street Fighter Talent

Kilzyou brings serious competitive pedigree to his new sponsor. The organization announced the roster move on social media with a direct hashtag referencing the summer event. This acquisition builds upon their established strategy for the international circuit. 

AG Global previously signed [Vxbao and Zhen](https://fgctopplayers.com/news/ag-global-signs-vxbao-and-zhen-for-ewc-club-championship/) to solidify their *Street Fighter 6* roster and maximize their win conditions. Organizations are actively prioritizing solo competitors who guarantee representation in specialized disciplines. Fielding a deep squad of top talent in a flagship game remains an efficient path to edging out rival clubs in the final standings.
