Itabashi Zangief, Dual Kevin, and EndingWalker all fell agonizingly short of the title twelve months ago. They now face a grueling three-day gauntlet starting May 22 to claim the crown for themselves.

## The Gauntlet: EWC Spots and CPT Premier Points

Winning in Schaumburg carries a unique prestige within the competitive ecosystem. The grueling Midwest major tests physical endurance just as much as technical execution. Competitors must survive a sprawling double-elimination bracket spanning three days to even reach the Sunday stage. The financial incentive matches the historical prestige. A $19,710 USD prize pool is on the line for Street Fighter 6.

The money is only part of the equation. Two direct qualification spots for the Esports World Cup 2026 will be awarded to the highest finishers. Players are also fighting for critical Capcom Pro Tour Premier points to secure their standing for Capcom Cup 13. 

The volume of 1,451 entrants means top seeds will face dangerous challengers early in the pool stages. Players must maintain strict focus through countless short sets before reaching the best-of-five matches in Top 8.

---

## The Favorites: Vengeance in Schaumburg

Itabashi Zangief defined what it meant to play Zangief at the highest level in 2025. He secured second place at this exact venue last year by suffocating opponents with relentless command grabs. His mastery of the character makes him a structural nightmare for players reliant on predictable offense, especially as the character sits in a strong position in the current competitive tier list.

Dual Kevin brings one of the highest FGPI scores in North America to the bracket. He placed third here last year and remains one of the most consistent competitors globally. He pilots a character with a strong tournament presence and forces opponents into exhausting defensive sequences. His ability to maintain relentless pressure across a long tournament run makes him a constant threat.

EndingWalker secured fourth place at the 2025 event and continues to refine his clinical neutral game. The young European standout utilizes spacing and whiff punishment to dismantle aggressive opponents. He relies on tight defensive fundamentals to navigate the early rounds.

---

## The Disruptors: Global Threats Seeking Top 8

International travelers present a severe roadblock for the returning North American favorites. The bracket features lethal specialists who excel in long tournaments. These competitors understand exactly how to pace themselves over a dense three-day schedule. 

Veterans like GO1 and xiaohai possess the defensive discipline required to slow down the aggressive styles prevalent in the local scene. Both players boast exceptional tournament endurance. Their presence creates a softer path through the winners side for whoever manages to avoid their quadrant of the bracket. A disciplined international player can easily exploit the chaotic early pools to secure a guaranteed Sunday appearance.

## The Pick: Closing the Deal

The sheer size of the Street Fighter 6 bracket makes predictions inherently difficult. The top seeds must survive a gauntlet of regional killers hungry for an upset. Every opponent has studied the footage of last year's Top 8 to find structural weaknesses in the favorites. 

Based on the numbers, Dual Kevin is our pick to finally secure the championship. His high FGPI score reflects a deep consistency in reaching the late stages of major events. He is well-equipped to navigate the early pools with minimal risk. If he can implement his smothering offense effectively on Sunday, he is a logical candidate to claim the vacant throne.
