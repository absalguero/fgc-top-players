MenaRD broke the Beast's legendary exhibition streak by taking down Daigo Umehara 10-6 in a grueling Kemonomichi FT10 set.

<div class="takeaways">

**Key Takeaways:**
* **MenaRD pulled away in the back half of the set after a tense 5-5 tie, adapting to Daigo's prep with suffocating defense and perfect parries.**
* **Daigo's performance defied expectations, proving his signature meticulous preparation still makes him a lethal threat in long sets.**
* **The victory cements MenaRD's claim as an elite force in Street Fighter 6 and marks the end of Daigo's historic undefeated run in the FT10 format.**

## The Kemonomichi Collision

The highly anticipated clash between the Dominican powerhouse and the Japanese god of fighting games delivered on its promise. Daigo spent nearly a month completely off the grid to prepare specifically for MenaRD’s suffocating pressure, even skipping his team's Street Fighter League championship celebration. That preparation showed early on as Daigo fought tooth and nail to bring the set to an even 5-5 tie. He defied the doubters who predicted a blowout, showcasing his unparalleled game knowledge and ability to exploit minute habits. 

However, Street Fighter 6's brutal mental stack eventually took its toll in the back half of the marathon set. As the match entered its final stretch, MenaRD’s ability to download his opponent mid-match became the deciding factor. The Dominican star adjusted to Daigo's setups, locking down the neutral game with flawless perfect parries and relentless Blanka offense. Daigo fought valiantly to weather the storm of Level 2 supers, but Mena’s youthful reaction time and unbreakable defense secured the final five games needed to close it out.

---

## Passing of the Torch

This 10-6 result marks a clear shift in the competitive landscape. For years, "Daigo with prep time" was considered an unbeatable monster in the FT10 format, claiming historic victories over legends like Tokido and Infiltration. MenaRD stepping up to shatter that barrier not only adds the ultimate trophy to his resume but solidifies his position as one of the most dominant players of the current era. 

Even in defeat, Daigo reminded the world exactly why he is FGC royalty. Taking six games off a generational talent like MenaRD proved his competitive fire is still burning bright. The match serves as a perfect encapsulation of Street Fighter 6's evolving ecosystem, where the old guard's mastery of fundamentals constantly collides with the explosive adaptations of the new generation.
