Five top Street Fighter 6 competitors concluded Alex possesses zero purely favorable matchups during a rigorous 60-minute strategy session.

<div class="takeaways">

**Key Takeaways:**
* **Zero outright advantages:** The brain trust could not identify a single character Alex mathematically beats in standard neutral interactions.
* **Stance dictates viability:** Characters lacking fireballs struggle severely against Alex's Prowler Stance mixups and armor.
* **Projectile despair:** Zoners and fireball-heavy kits completely invalidate the grappler's approach options and stance pressure.

</div>

---

## The Prowler Stance Paradigm

During a comprehensive collaborative session published, a syndicate of veteran players mapped out the exact tournament viability of Alex. The group included Dogura, Nemo, Pugera, KEI.B, and Hotdog. They immediately encountered a glaring problem when attempting to fill out the top tier of their chart. The competitors openly admitted they could not name a single explicitly favorable matchup for the grappler. This realization forced them to label the highest tier "Hope" rather than a traditional advantage.

Nemo provided the strongest argument for Alex's competitive survival. He demonstrated that relying heavily on the Prowler Stance completely alters neutral interactions. Hovering in this stance forces characters without invincible approaches or projectiles to second-guess their offensive pressure. Fighters like Honda and Lily drop into the higher tiers on the chart simply because they lack reliable tools to break this specific defensive posture.

---

## The Projectile Problem

The bottom of the tier list paints a much bleaker picture. The absolute lowest category is reserved almost exclusively for characters who can bypass the stance strategy entirely. The group noted that strong fireball games from characters like Terry and JP make approach attempts feel nearly impossible. Projectiles force Alex out of his stance and demand dangerous jump-ins or highly risky parry attempts to close the distance.

Despite the bloated unfavorable tiers spanning the middle of the chart, these top competitors remain optimistic. All five players have extensive experience piloting M. Bison at a high level. They specifically debated how to frame Alex's viability to the public before agreeing he currently offers more strategic possibilities than the Shadaloo dictator. Alex undoubtedly suffers in neutral engagements across the cast. His explosive robbery potential still gives players a tangible win condition when their back is against the wall.

---

## Future Meta Implications

The reliance on this stance strategy creates a sharp divide in the competitive landscape. Characters equipped with strong projectiles will continue to gatekeep the grappler out of the top tiers. Fighters lacking those ranged options must develop entirely new approach angles to bypass his armor. Competitors adopting Alex now have a clear blueprint for which matchups to hunt and which to avoid entirely during their bracket runs.
